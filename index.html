01 About

I started out doing fieldwork for Environmental Impact Assessments in Bangladesh — collecting water samples, running focus group discussions with people whose land sat in the path of a new railway line, and writing the reports that decide whether a project gets its environmental clearance. Over six years that turned into leading EIA/ESIA studies for some of the country's largest donor-funded infrastructure programs, from the Dhaka BRT corridor to gas pipeline expansions and railway conversions under the ADB and JICA.

In 2025 I moved to Enschede to start an MSc in Spatial Engineering at the University of Twente's ITC faculty. I'm building on the fieldwork and impact-assessment background with formal training in GIS, remote sensing, and disaster risk modeling — the tools I was reaching for informally on projects like the Sandwip Island morphological assessment and the coastal afforestation study I published in 2024.

Outside of coursework, I'm currently President of Orbit, the ITC student association at the University of Twente, which keeps me in the same kind of coordination-and-people-management role I had running field survey teams back in Dhaka.
